date: 2024-03-01
topics: [writing, design]
summary: "Type choices are content choices. The font, the weight, the spacing — all of it signals how a reader should feel before they read a single word."
---

Typography is not decoration applied after writing is finished. It is part of the writing. When you choose a serif over a sans-serif, you are making a claim about the register of the text — formal or casual, slow or fast, warm or clinical.

The variables that matter most are the ones readers don't consciously notice: line length, line height, the size relationship between headings and body text. When these are calibrated well, readers move through text without friction. When they're off, readers feel a vague discomfort they may attribute to the writing itself.

Source Serif 4 was designed specifically for long-form reading at screen resolutions. The optical sizing axis adjusts letterform details automatically based on the rendered size — slightly more contrast and sharper serifs at large display sizes, more generous spacing and gentler curves at small body sizes. The same mechanics that make metal type work across sizes are now built into the font file.
